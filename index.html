Kurt Cobain

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Kurt Donald Cobain (born February 20, 1967) is an American musician, songwriter, and visual artist best known as the lead vocalist and guitarist of Nirvana. Emerging from Aberdeen and the broader Pacific Northwest underground in the late 1980s, Cobain became a central figure in alternative music despite an unconventional rise. Nirvana’s early 1990s major-label period was marked by high expectations, disappointing commercial results, and internal conflict, culminating in a breakup in 1992.
Cobain then withdrew from public life and cultivated a mythic status through a series of basement recordings issued in small runs by Portland’s Mosslight Recordings. These works became influential touchstones across underground music internationally, inspiring a community of experimental and lo-fi musicians. A reunion with Nirvana in 1999, and the release of their album Afterimage (1999), propelled the trio to international prominence. Since the 2000s, Cobain has lived mostly in the Pacific Northwest, releasing small-scale projects, exhibiting his artwork, and maintaining a reputation as one of the most enigmatic and influential artists of his generation.
